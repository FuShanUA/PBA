In Crisis Response, Answer the Simple Questions First

Robert Fink
5 min read
·
Mar 30, 2020

Being quarantined in my Munich apartment as my Palantir colleagues around the world spring into action to help our customers respond to the COVID-19 pandemic has been an opportunity to reflect on the lessons we have learned as a company over a decade of supporting both crisis response and public health. Palantir has provided our data integration and analysis platforms for urgent public health and disaster relief work through many of our partnerships, whether on long-term cancer research or in-the-moment responses to natural disasters. We have identified a number of cultural and technology practices that are especially valuable in a time of crisis. I hope that explaining these will be helpful to organizations facing circumstances changing dramatically from hour to hour and to engineers participating in hackathons to support COVID-19 response.

Crisis response requires velocity and pragmatism. Importantly, this does not mean that we can or should ignore architectural principles such as privacy protection, data security, or governance (see below).
