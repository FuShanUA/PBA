Quality journalism for the digital age

Putting data into action

A decade ago, many commentators forecasted the decline of traditional journalism. Today at Axel Springer, quality media remains a viable business model thanks to the power of data.

Online advertising and paid subscriptions are now the bedrock of their business, yet with social media and freely accessible online content, delivering high quality new demands data-driven strategies. Creating a digital-first organization also brings with it a new set of internal challenges: how do you connect teams with the correct data sets, how do you make data accessible to non-technical employees, and most importantly, how do you ensure it can be shared securely throughout the organization?

Building a data-driven media company

ML for the modern newsroom

Using Palantir Foundry to integrate data from across their different titles and revenue streams, Axel Springer has built a more agile, data-driven publishing organization that can quickly adapt and respond to trends in consumer behaviors and interests.

Foundry’s advanced AI and machine learning capabilities have allowed Axel Springer to develop granular insights into readership behavior, advertising performance and their subscription models. As a result, they can not only continue to offer engaging and competitively priced content, but refine and expand their revenue streams through these new insights.

Empowering free decisions

Article analytics

By developing machine learning models in Foundry, Axel Springer can compare the performance of stories to comparative baselines, and better gauge what content their readers value and like.

Subscriptions

Through the integration of subscription data into the platform, they can better understand the likelihood of subscription conversion and retention, as well as more accurately predict customer lifetime values.

Advertising

Axel Springer has also used Foundry to leverage minimum pricing and real-time bidding for advertising slots across their publications to optimize revenue.
