On The Front Lines

The World Food Programme (WFP) operates one of the most complex, consequential supply chains on Earth — deploying thousands of trucks, aircraft, and ships to deliver life-saving assistance to over 100 million people each year.

To meet their mandate of Zero Hunger, WFP reaches some of the most remote corners of the world and responds to dire emergencies.

From natural disasters to famine to conflict, WFP is on the front lines bringing assistance to those who need it most.

Data Makes A Difference

When every minute could mean the difference between life and death, WFP needs to ensure that individuals across the organization are empowered with the right data to make informed decisions as quickly as possible.

Since 2019, WFP has partnered with Palantir to integrate many existing systems into a collaborative, operational platform called DOTS, enabling staff to rapidly surface the information they need, anticipate bottlenecks in advance, and identify alternative paths to continue delivering. 

DOTS also facilitates better decision-making among team members. Every time a field officer solves a logistics problem within DOTS, the decision can be written-back into the underlying common data asset, helping optimize future logistics.

"We have a saying internally that says, ‘nothing stands between WFP and a hungry child.’ That’s where we turn to the power of data. Because of the power of DOTS, we are going to have an organization that is more intelligent, that is faster, that is more responsive to the needs of the communities we serve."

↳ Pierre Guillaume Wielezynski, Head of Digital Transformation Services at WFP

Combating COVID-19

When COVID-19 struck, businesses and supply chains around the world ground to a halt — but WFP expanded operations.

Per the UN’s COVID-19 Global Humanitarian Response Plan, humanitarian organizations across the globe utilized WFP’s supply chain to deliver emergency health and humanitarian assistance.

Using the Control Tower module in DOTS, WFP was able to track and update these shipments in real time, delivering more than 148,000 cubic meters of life-saving health and humanitarian cargo to 173 countries on behalf of 72 organizations in response to the pandemic, and save $30M in operations.

In 2021, WFP was awarded the 2021 INFORMS Edelman Award for its use of analytics and data to improve emergency food assistance.
