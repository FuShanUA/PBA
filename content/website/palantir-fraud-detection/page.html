Overview

Fraud detection holds significant importance across various domains due to its impact on financial stability, organizational integrity, and consumer trust. Palantir Foundry can provide configurable, on-going monitoring for anomalous transactions, activities, and behaviors to help organizations promptly identify and address potential fraud risks.

By leveraging advanced analytics and AI/ML models, Foundry can enhance the accuracy and efficiency of fraud detection processes, ultimately safeguarding assets and maintaining trust among stakeholders.

Palantir Foundry can accommodate data of any kind or volume and automate many of the data integration, transformation, alerting, and reporting functions that typically consume thousands of hours of analyst time.

The Foundry Case Manager

The Foundry Case Manager guides and tracks investigation steps through resolution and reporting. Each alert incorporates all features from scenario, customer, counterparties, underlying transactions, jurisdictions, and users can easily drill down to determine contributing alert factors.

Foundry automatically categorizes risk-scored alerts into outcome probabilities (e.g., L1 disposition, L2 escalation, etc.) to aid investigation, enabling analysts to focus on high-risk alerts while process automation can automatically discard low-risk alerts. Foundry can alert the relevant users when automatically discarded alerts significantly increase/decrease overall or within specific scenarios.

AI/ML risk models receive feedback from all human decisions (manual decision to discard an alert) to retrain and improve performance over time. Foundry can also share alerts with external systems and/or block transactions as required.

Foundry includes comprehensive dashboard and report-building capabilities, enabling organizations to visualize and monitor for common fraud concerns using:

  • Key fraud metrics: gross loss, net loss to Customer, net loss to 3rd-party payment providers, refund value, payments that missed release cut-off by fraud category and type, etc.)

  • Operational metrics: alerts open/closed, transaction volume, etc.)

  • Fraud KPIs: user/team activity, speed/accuracy, rule/ML performance, etc.)

  • Reports: Regulatory, whitelisting and blacklisting reports
