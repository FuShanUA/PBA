Noto Peninsula Earthquake

Deploying Advanced Data Integration to Aid Disaster Response Efforts.

Overview

On January 1, 2024, a devastating earthquake struck the Noto Peninsula in Ishikawa Prefecture, Japan, accompanied by a tsunami, severe flooding, electricity outages, and water outages. The disaster damaged 193,529 structures across nine prefectures, and evacuation orders affected 62,000 people who were relocated to temporary shelters.

Recognizing Palantir’s previous experience in the local government space supporting Kanagawa Prefecture during the COVID-19 pandemic, Ishikawa Prefecture engaged Palantir to deploy advanced data integration and management solutions to aid disaster response efforts. Specifically, Palantir technology was used to integrate dispersed evacuee data, and manage personal information securely.

Solution

Palantir immediately established four core objectives:

  • Enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of disaster response and relief efforts.

  • Develop a standardized data model for evacuee management.

  • Support the Joint Task Force in prioritizing disaster victim support operations.

  • Facilitate seamless collaboration across municipalities for evacuee management.

Within 7 weeks, Palantir engineers delivered:

The Disaster Victim Database

Launched on February 24, 2024, the Disaster Victim Database integrates various data sources, including Prefecture systems, LINE App data, and external systems from NPOs. This platform allows authorities to efficiently track the locations and needs of evacuees.

The Disaster Victim Database was particularly helpful to track and support residents who had chosen to evacuate independently following the earthquake.

The Common Operating Picture (COP)

In response to the 2024 Noto Peninsula Heavy Rain that followed, Palantir integrated environmental data (e.g., roadblocks, landslides, satellite imagery) with village location data to develop a Common Operating Picture (COP). This tool was utilized by the Joint Task Force in prioritizing disaster victim support operations.

The COP enabled municipalities and support organizations to share information quickly and securely, allowing them to appropriately access relevant information.

Dynamic Evacuee Tracking and Management

Palantir integrated data from internal systems and external services to create a dynamic access control system that manages evacuees as they move across municipalities. This system ensures evacuees receive the necessary support and prevents gaps in service provision.

The "Disaster Victim 360" system was developed by integrating 15 data sources, providing up-to-date information on disaster victims. This system plays a revolutionary role in aggregating and providing information during disasters while ensuring personal data protection.

Specifically, Palantir collaborated with the Personal Information Protection Commission to implement strict access controls in compliance with the law, deploying a feature that dynamically adjusts access controls based on the location of victim.

Impact

Palantir's involvement has significantly enhanced the disaster response capabilities in Japan:

  • Improved Decision-Making:

    The Disaster Victim Database and COP have empowered authorities to make informed decisions quickly, reducing response times and optimizing resource allocation.

  • Standardized Data Management:

    The collaboration with the Cabinet Office has led to the development of a national standardized data model for future disaster responses, ensuring consistency and efficiency across regions.

  • Enhanced Collaboration:

    The dynamic evacuee management system has facilitated better coordination among municipalities, ensuring comprehensive support for evacuees.

  • Rapid Response:

    The disaster victim data model can be reused in other projects. For example, during heavy rain in the Noto Peninsula in September 2024, an app was built within 24 hours to support disaster relief, demonstrating the model's flexibility and effectiveness.

Conclusion

Palantir's work in the Ishikawa Prefecture demonstrates the transformative power of data integration and management in disaster response efforts. By providing advanced technological solutions, Palantir has played a crucial role in enhancing the resilience and responsiveness of local authorities, ultimately improving the safety and well-being of affected communities.

This partnership not only underscores Palantir's commitment to leveraging data-driven solutions in crisis situations but also sets a precedent for how technology can be harnessed to enhance disaster management efforts globally, offering a scalable model for other regions facing similar challenges.
