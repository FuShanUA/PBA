Minimizing Wind Turbine Downtime

A wind park operator recently used Foundry to conduct large-scale diagnostics of the pitch angle of the blades on individual wind turbines.

Solution

With Foundry's Quiver, performance engineers were able to analyze the pitch angle time curves for 10-20 turbines at a time and use those to diagnose whether the blades are pitching optimally.

Using Quiver, for the first time performance engineers were able to create pitch angle curves from wind speed and pitch angle time series. They were able to compare 10-20 of these created curves at once to diagnose whether certain turbines were pitching differently than the group.

One analysis uncovered blades pitching at different angles, vibrating the turbine and causing stress on some of the different components.

This stress can expedite the need for turbine maintenance. This analysis was then applied across all available turbines, grouping by similar technologies to surface any other potential issues. This allowed the team to minimize downtime from high vibration.

Impact

  • Analysis in Foundry identified that half of the turbines at one of the client’s largest wind sites had blades that were misaligned.

  • Based on this analysis, the customer was empowered to contact the OEM, change blade operations and drive down costs.
