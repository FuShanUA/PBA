Integrating ERP Intelligence

Challenge

A global energy company is actively working to reduce $2 billion in inventory costs by 50%. The data needed to achieve this goal, however, resides in more than five legacy ERP systems, preventing ongoing analysis and optimization of material orders across systems. 

One obstacle is that many ERP systems store data natively. For example, data on raw materials alone are stored in 50+ tables in SAP systems. Relationships between the tables are complex and data definitions are hard to establish. These native formats are illegible to most consumers and can only be interpreted after months of training.

Historically, preparing data for analysis has taken months, required teams of consultants, and driven up cloud costs. The time-intensive process entails defining filters to select the appropriate data, writing custom scripts to extract the right tables, and building reports for relevant business units. 

As a result, most of the time and resources related to optimizing working capital has been devoted to sourcing, cleaning, and compiling data, rather than analyzing it to power decisions.

Solution

For the first time, ERP data is self-serve and interpretable to decision makers, who can now focus on deriving business insights instead of performing costly data preparation. Teams use a code-free application in Palantir ERP Suite to:

  • Visually identify and select source data – without knowledge of underlying tables.

  • Automate ingestion from source systems, build pipelines, and prepare data for analysis by mapping it to the enterprise Foundry ontology

  • Instantly configure out-of-the-box workflows to power analysis and decision making

  • Today, teams work directly with ERP data in Foundry without having to navigate complex data interactions in ERP systems, while reducing cloud costs for data preparation.

With all ERP data in Foundry, an alerting workflow identifies surplus materials and flags when material orders at one plant can be met with existing surplus elsewhere. This workflow and others allow the business to dramatically reduce inventory expenses and optimize working capital.

Impact

Within weeks of installing the ERP Suite, it was able to unlock approximately $50 million in working capital and has the potential to unlock further savings if applied across their worldwide supply chain. 

Since reports no longer need to be built by code, customer teams can consume and analyze ERP data significantly faster.
