Through Palantir’s principal membership with the SOSA Consortium and The Open Group, we’ll contribute to the Consortium’s efforts - in alignment with the MOSA directive from the United States Department of Defense - to collaboratively develop open standards and best practices across government and industry organizations.

Why MOSA?

Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA) enables components of complex systems to be competed separately to procure affordable, adaptable, and capable systems in a rapidly evolving threat environment.

MOSA enables these systems to continuously harness technological innovation to remain competitive against peer adversaries.

Palantir’s Approach

Palantir solutions bolster MOSA frameworks to provide capability that enables deployment of innovative technology more easily, cost effectively, and rapidly across complex Army solution ecosystems. 

How do we do it?

Modular, microservices-based design architecture:

This provides highly cohesive capability that is loosely coupled. This means microservices can be turned on/off for specific use cases and new capabilities can be substituted in as they come online.

Interfaces provided with open APIs:

These APIs conform to industry standards, all defined for developers and users via publicly available documentation.

Use of consensus-based standards:

This includes open-source languages and data formats.

Core Capabilities

At Palantir, we implement MOSA principles into our government solutions, from the foundation up. Our software offerings comprise multiple capability modules, which we have designed to be highly cohesive, loosely coupled, extensible, interoperable, and fully severable.

Why is this critical? Palantir can augment existing systems and enable outcomes today in an end-to-end solution, a highly specific capability, or anything in between. This enables organizations and users to:

  • Focus on simplifying the complexity and delivering flexibility to modern software ecosystems

  • Minimize service hours, time, and cost required to add, remove, or replace severable modular components.

  • Reduce schedule and cost risk for capability modifications.

  • Ensure cost efficacy and ease to extend and maintain by Government and third parties.

  • Minimize bottlenecks to capability fielding and systems integration to keep pace with innovation.
