Operating the Largest Underground Natural Gas Storage Business in the U.S.

As a critical energy infrastructure company in North America, Kinder Morgan operates the largest underground natural gas storage business in the United States, with a working gas storage capacity of up to 700 billion cubic feet. This storage capacity combined with the company's robust pipeline network connections allows it to reliably serve millions of customers through the coldest winters and hottest summers.

Given their critical role in providing energy access, Kinder Morgan is focused on continually improving operations and gaining new insights into energy markets. However, challenges ranging from volatile energy demands to disparate datasets can create the potential for inefficiencies, time-consuming processes and delayed responses.

Building a Decision Support Tool for the Texas Energy Market with Foundry

Kinder Morgan deployed Foundry to help address these challenges. The company’s Foundry proof of concept was to gain insight into the Texas energy market and improve operations in an ever-changing environment. To do this, they set out to create a seven-day look-ahead decision support tool DST.

The Texas energy market is highly volatile, with massive load swings – intraday power demands can go from 35,000 MW to 80,000 MW – and wide fluctuations in the availability of wind power supply, which can drop from 30,000 MW to 1,000 MW in hours. Given this unpredictability, the Electric Reliability Council of Texas (ERCOT) relies on quick start natural gas-fired turbines to stabilize the power grid; Kinder Morgan meets rapid power demand for fuel with natural gas from its underground storage facilities.

Predicting when demand would suddenly increase or decrease was an art Kinder Morgan had developed over many years. Engineers, marketers, and analysts spent hours collecting weather, power demand and power supply data to ensure the pipelines and gas storage facilities were set up to meet market demands.

Kinder Morgan’s first Foundry use case involved integrating multiple data sources to create a DST for their midstream underground natural gas storage business. Before Foundry, this integration took two to three hours and required pulling data from multiple sites; now, data is programmatically extracted and transformed into actionable insights within two to three minutes.

Today, Kinder Morgan’s DST, created in Foundry, combines weather data and the power forecast to predict gas demand for the Texas market, improving the company’s ability to meet market demands for rapid response fuel.

Impact

  • Consolidated view of the next seven days of market demand, supply, weather drivers and Kinder Morgan real-time response capabilities

  • Savings of two to three hours per day of market analysis of weather, demand and supply data

  • Synchronized, cross-department view of market and Kinder Morgan operations using same data sources

  • Quantitative view of supply and demand (versus historical qualitative view)

  • Maintenance activities planned for likely lower demand days

  • Faster response to market demands by configuring facilities to meet future demand before energy is needed

  • Data consistency and speed of extraction improves response to market needs
