Optimizing Batch Production Processes

Partner

  • A Top 5 Global Pharma Client

Problem Space

  • Reduced processed yields in an important product production line. Each component in the production line had sensors generating valuable data, but due to the scale, process experts were struggling to make use of it. Data on batch quality was stored in a separate data warehouse, making analysis with data on process conditions difficult.

Impact

  • Seamless data integration between batch data and sensor data enabled process experts to correlate historical process conditions that led to 12 % higher yields. This amounted to $100Ms of additional revenue potential.

  • Process experts were able to test their hypothesis through decision simulations in Foundry and have higher confidence in their proposed parameters prior to make changes in their production process.

  • Daily monitoring of batch quality enabled near real time detection of any new deviations, and allowed for better understanding on the behaviors of reactions, providing broader visibility into the process landscape.

Yield Optimization

Partner

  • Pharma client

Problem Space

  • High variability of biologic product yield for key clinical programs. Manually investigating individual batches was time consuming and often ultimately inconclusive. Teams across the company had limited ability for collaborative analysis, and there was no mechanism for conducting confirmatory experiments. The ultimate clinical impact of yield metrics was unclear, and it was difficult to derive next steps to maximize the effective of the new therapy

Impact

  • Historical manufacturing yields were matched with raw material metadata, allowing for automated analysis for continued monitoring on unexpected relationships between input material and production quality.

  • With more historical data, analysis could be completed by machine learning models to spot deviations earlier and provide enhanced projections of realized yield, informing improved resource allocation.

  • Collaboration was improved through automatic integration of assay results from both in-house teams and CMO partners, creating an end-to-end record of all batches.

Resilient Supply Chain

Partner

  • Client developing novel cell therapies

Problem Space

  • Need for a supply chain that was both flexible and highly controlled to account for increasingly frequent disruptions such as raw material shortages, vendor backlogs, and other shocks, without compromising quality. This was challenging as teams across the company operated in siloed systems with little visibility into the downstream impact of any system changes.

Impact

  • Integration of disparate ERP inventory systems, IoT environmental sensors, and CMO assays (in-process and release) resulted in a single source of truth record about the progression of a cell product.

  • Alerts, both threshold rules and ML-model based, ensured that troubling patterns in a batch are caught with time to intervene. As conditions evolve, projections of product supply automatically adjust to inform planning.

  • Scenario analysis tools allow supply chain teams to experiment with adjusting raw materials and resource allocation to limit the impact of disruptions and adapt to changes in clinical demand.
