About Us

Palantir was founded in 2003 to help solve critical data issues in the U.S. intelligence community while protecting civil liberties. We later applied our learnings from frontline deployments to support commercial enterprises.

Today, organizations around the world deploy our platforms — Foundry, Gotham, and Apollo — for their most important work; our software powers AI-assisted decision-making from war zones to factory floors.

Palantir Foundry is our operating system for the modern enterprise. Built to deliver results with the speed of an out-of-the-box solution and the flexibility to fit unique businesses and use cases, Foundry connects organizations’ data, analytics, and operations, fueling responsible, fully governed AI-powered operations. 

At its core is the Foundry Ontology. The Foundry Ontology is a full-fidelity, dynamic representation of your business and the world around it. It is shared by your entire organization, enabling coordination and interconnectivity across data, modeling, analytics, operational decisions, and end-user workflows. 

With the Foundry Ontology, users can chain together models across workflows, teams, and organizational units to simulate operations and optimize decisions, as well as to orchestrate model-driven actions across existing systems.

  • This empowers engineers to conduct analyses ranging from run length optimization to predictive maintenance while enabling higher-level analyses, such as connecting detailed production processes with executive decision-making.

Empowering Process Engineers

Refinery engineers can create their own tools using data in Foundry and its analytical tooling, including:

  • Unit health monitoring Quiver dashboards and wink sheets to ensure safe operations.

  • Constraints optimization dashboards to automatically alert engineers to constraint breaches, helping teams identify sub-optimal production areas and identify optimization opportunities and risks.

Simulation

  • Engineers can build process flow diagrams in Foundry using the plant ontology. These objects can be linked to external physics-based models such as HYSYS.

  • Engineers can then pose “what-if” questions to test and optimize set points, run times, and maintenance plans. Theoretical values can be compared with actuals to identify potential issues.

  • Foundry’s timeseries suite enables engineers to construct synthetic timeseries using historical values, model outputs, and first-principle equations.

  • Complex machine learning techniques to standardized regressions can be executed to forecast expected future performance.

CAPEX Planning

  • Combine historic data with plant infrastructure and streaming sensor data to evaluate and monitor turnaround plans, identify problem areas, improve operational uptime and throughput, and optimize commercial production.

  • Foundry gives engineers a singular, real-time source of truth, enabling refineries to adhere to production planning.

  • Compare and understand actual refinery performance versus planned performance.

  • Minimize extreme production deviations by alerting engineers whenever there is a significant deviation to production.
