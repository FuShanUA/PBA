新科技复兴时代

硅谷诞生于美国腹地。它源自一个在边疆开拓中孕育的国家，由那些敢于缔造伟大工程、以匹配伟大国家与同胞期许的人所创造。

作为美国人，我们是建设者、技术专家和先驱的传承者。今天，我们将莱特兄弟、亨利·福特、查克·叶格、海曼·里科弗和鲍勃·诺伊斯尊为英雄。而在他们所处的时代，他们却是异类——自满的敌人、现状的颠覆者……甚至是有些冒犯的、毫不妥协的爱国者。

在我们所熟知的这些“异端”英雄背后，还有成千上万同样具备这些美德、为相同目标奋斗、值得同等自豪的人。这就是美国工人的故事。

美国工人缔造了20世纪。他们铸造了“民主兵工厂”，将星条旗插上月球表面，推翻了一个帝国，并掀起了一场数字革命。

但美国逐渐变得骄奢。

一种有害的犬儒主义悄然渗透进我们的文化、政治和企业之中。各行各业相继沦为一种与真正的创新和雄心背道而驰的意识形态的牺牲品。美国抛弃了她的工人。工作岗位被转移至海外，本土的创新遭受打压。随之而来的是停滞不前。曾有人向我们承诺会拥有飞行汽车……

如今，人工智能的降临被吹捧为摆脱这种技术停滞的救星。对某些人而言，追求通用人工智能已演变为一场宗教式的探索，甚至承诺将我们从人类本性中拯救出来。

这场新人工智能宗教的某些先知鼓吹重返 paradise：永生，无需劳动，无需辛劳。这是一种空洞的生存状态，生命失去了意义与使命。

另有人预言人工智能将带来末日浩劫，警告这种超人类智能将凶残地反噬其创造者。他们声称，人类与人工智能的关系将始于剥削，演变为压迫，最终走向毁灭。

我们拒绝这些反乌托邦式的未来。我们坚信，美国——其文化与人民——在构建人工智能并从中受益方面具备得天独厚的优势。

这份信念，是我们凭实力赢来的。

我们屡屡目睹，前线和工厂车间里的男男女女正在推动人工智能最具变革性的应用。他们来自各行各业，技术背景各异。但他们的共同点在于对构建事物的直觉——一种与生俱来的艺术感，并在应对关键任务的挑战中不断打磨与展现。

这些经历进一步印证了我们的信念：人工智能必须以人为本，服务于人类本身，放大其独特天赋，并为其事业提供支持。

正是怀揣着这一愿景，我们创立了美国科技奖学金计划。

这项计划的意义远不止于职业发展——它旨在重塑那份承诺：只要勤奋刻苦、意志坚定，依然能在美国改变自身命运。它是对美国卓越精神的投资，印证了我们坚信美国最顶尖的建设者们依然身处各处，亟待发掘的信念。

我们始终相信，只要赋予合适的工具与机会，美国工人便是我国最大的竞争优势。是时候证明这一点了。

奖学金计划

Palantir 创立了美国科技奖学金计划，旨在为美国工人武装相关 技能 (Skills) 与机遇，从而彻底变革我们在美国的构建方式。奖学金获得者将参与一项高强度的培训课程，旨在将平庸之辈与真正的卓越之才区分开来。

表现优异的毕业生将直接获得与全国顶尖雇主对接的机会，这些雇主正是其所急需之人才。

申请者：唤醒沉睡的巨人。

  • 高强度培训：亲手体验 Palantir 业界领先的软件，学习正在塑造美国未来的工具。

  • 就业安置机会：表现优异的毕业生将有机会参加 Palantir 及其全国客户和合作伙伴的面试。

  • 无需技术学位：我们要寻找的是对结果有着极致执着的高主观能动性建设者——而不是只会粉饰简历的人。

Meet the Fellows

From thousands of applicants, 150 have been selected for their drive, ingenuity, and practical wisdom. Our initial cohorts include:

  • Transitioning military veterans and enlisted leaders

  • Engineers, operators, and autodidacts from American factories, railroads, logistics, and field service

  • Startup founders and self-taught technologists

What unites them:

  • A relentless commitment to learning, building, and solving the problems that matter. They come from the heartland and the coasts, from garages and machine shops, and they’re ready to drive America’s renewal.

Capstone Projects:

  • Fellows will complete a capstone project—an end-to-end operational workflow, dashboard, or automation—demonstrating their ability to bring AI to bear on real-world challenges.

Cody Sumner

Electrical Utilities

Cody enlisted in the U.S. Navy straight out of high school after 9/11, a decision born of duty and conviction. Coming from generations of electricians, he returned from service to build America's electrical infrastructure, working hands-on in power plants and substations across the country. His years on production floors taught him what the engineers in their offices could not: how to eliminate every wasteful click, every redundant task, every hour stolen from skilled workers by outdated systems.

On ATF: “The tools are so intuitive that Palantir has within the Foundry platform that I feel like it's almost easier to take a subject matter expert in an area and teach them the tools than it is to take a technical person that knows them the tools and try to teach them the industry. I think that’s the real value of this program.”

Ed Dziwak

Food Production & Distribution

Ed served as a personnelman in the U.S. Navy before building a career orchestrating supply chains for some of America's largest food brands. From steel to Gatorade, he's spent years translating chaos into consensus: refining demand signals, closing budget gaps, and presenting strategic forecasts to leadership teams. His vision: populate Foundry with every data point that matters, enabling decision-makers to plan production with a precision the industry has never seen.

The Palantir Advantage: "A lot of the manual work is automated, so we're spending more time making strategic decisions vs. daily executional tasks."

Michael Steien

Agriculture

Michael grew up in a town of 700 in northern Minnesota. After studying information systems, he became the IT manager for a family-run potato operation in Grand Forks, North Dakota, where he inherited complete data anarchy. Spreadsheets, disconnected systems, information silos. He spent years asking himself a simple question: Am I actually adding value? That existential doubt led him to Palantir, to ATF, and to building solutions that work. Michael’s commitment to service extends beyond the farm: he's also an officer in the North Dakota National Guard.

Goals for ATF: "I'm really open-minded with what I get out of this. I know that Palantir's name means something in the tech world. I'm ready and willing to learn."

Mason Hennings

Civil Engineering

Mason grew up visiting heavy civil construction sites in central Louisiana with his father, a surveyor. After studying construction engineering, he joined the heavy civil industry and discovered something troubling: America builds its most critical infrastructure using methods from 20 years ago. Hundreds of hours every week maintaining archaic systems. Billions of dollars in lessons learned, never captured. He believes that the American construction industry is on the verge of a technological revolution that will transform how we use data to plan and execute major infrastructure projects.

On Rebuilding American Infrastructure: "What I'm here to do at the Fellowship is to not only validate the vision that I see for how we can actually build the future of some of the most important infrastructure in America, but to spread that vision across as many companies as I can to show them that there's a better way and you're losing money otherwise."

Jay Baker

Military Medicine

Jay spent 21 years as a U.S. Army physician, serving four times as a brigade surgeon and treating wounded soldiers on frontlines across the globe. His work with Palantir in the military sparked a realization: if AI can revolutionize intelligence and targeting, it can do the same for combat casualty care. He joined ATF to build that future, one where medics have the resources they need, when they need them, and where predictive technology saves not just thousands of lives, but hundreds of thousands.

On protecting America's Warfighters: "I had this vision that AI and machine learning should be able to do for combat casualty care what it does for intelligence and targeting. So I started to think about and talk with my planners how we were going to make this better. Translating all of that into data has saved thousands of lives and now we have to make an off-the-shelf capability that others can use everywhere."
