Your Business in Full Fidelity.

Foundry’s Ontology transforms your digital assets — including data, models, and processes — into a dynamic, actionable representation of the business for all users to leverage in operations:

  • Objects digitally represent the real-world entities, relationships and events, that constitute your business.

  • Relations represent the connections between real world entities, events and processes.

  • Actions capture the kinetics between objects and orchestrate real world change through your enterprise systems. Actions can be mapped from existing processes or existing models.

Pipeline Builder

Foundry’s flagship Ontology Hydration capability, for robust scalable pipelines at speed.

  • Build production-grade pipelines with Pipeline Builder using a dynamic point-and-click interface. Start by defining endpoint schema for Ontology object types and properties and describing the pipeline to match inputs to endpoints. Pipeline Builder’s back end will then automatically write transform code to build the desired pipelines.

  • Pipeline Builder works with all types of data (structured, unstructured, IoT, geospatial et al.) across all modalities (batch, micro-batch, streaming, et al.).

Native Federation

Incorporate existing datasets in external systems into objects — without duplicating the underlying data.

  • Integrate directly with data platforms, streamlining the hydration of existing data assets into the Ontology.

  • Federation ensures that the Ontology can harness the full power of your enterprise architecture, without fracturing existing sources of truth.

Model Hydration

Capture the kinetics of your organization. Interoperable model connectors, integrations for externally hosted models, and built-in tooling make model hydration seamless.

Ontology Manager

Design your Ontology with maximum precision to fit the context of your organization.

  • Easily define your organization’s Objects, as well as their Links, Properties (including time series and geospatial properties), and metadata.

  • Specify the key decisions and behavior in your organization, with rails for mapping models to Actions that users or systems can implement — closing the loop between AI/ML and operations.

  • Compose Functions that determine how Objects should be updated, how external notifications will work, and how user-applied Actions write to APIs outside of Foundry. Functions can also compute variables, derived properties, and derived aggregations for use in applications.

Model Objectives

Orient your models around specific operational outcomes within an environment designed to streamline model management and deployment.

  • Bind your models to the Ontology with Model Objectives. Binding connects models with analytics, operational applications, API subscribers, scenario analysis, or broader simulations. It also allows user-facing applications to perform Actions that propose or commit real operational changes within the platform, or push to operational systems outside of Foundry.

  • Model Objectives allow you to define a specific modeling problem in terms of an operational outcome, creating a communication hub for your modeling efforts and a system of record for evaluating, reviewing, and operationalizing successive model solutions over time.

  • Configure writeback pathways to capture Actions and feedback as new data and implement changes to systems of record. Writeback provides business and modeling teams with a powerful data asset for monitoring, understanding, and improving production performance, as well as identifying and adapting to new circumstances.

Ontology Process Orchestration

Model, diagnose, and automate business processes within the Ontology for unprecedented scale, reusability, and speed to value.

  • Define business processes — including their tasks, transitions, and teams — in a first-class way within the Ontology, either from an existing business process model or from scratch.

  • Read existing process logs and correlate process steps to process outcomes. Define ideal process pathing to identify gaps, bottlenecks, and high-impact decisions, as well as to configure monitoring.

  • Configure process execution and automation in the Ontology by linking Actions with process models to create state transformations and execute tasks.

Foundry Rules

Define and codify business logic that drives critical workflows.

  • Create and apply rules to Objects, datasets, and time series data with a point-and-click, low-code interface for a variety of use cases like real-time process monitoring and alerting.

  • Configure rule notifications. Rules can surface alerts in-platform and integrate with incident response software like PagerDuty to trigger text messages and other communications to decision makers. 

  • Connect models to alerts, allowing models to suggest remediations to users or even automatically action alerts, increasing response rate in time-sensitive situations.

Application Building

For novel applications and use cases, Foundry comes with app building capabilities for users from all backgrounds.

  • Workshop empowers a broad range of users to create Ontology-aware applications with flexible building blocks from no-code, low-code, and fully customizable code-based widgets. Leverage the full breadth of your Ontology’s objects and actions for deep interactivity, a tailored user experience, and operational efficacy.

  • Slate allows app builders to construct dynamic, bespoke applications at speed, allowing for full customization using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Seamless integration with the Ontology allows for deep visibility into the relationships and interdependencies between Objects and Actions, helping you build custom applications that fully fit any unmet operational need.

Object-Aware Analytics

Groundbreaking analytic applications in Foundry are fully object-driven, allowing non-technical users to self-serve in the language of the business for even the most technically demanding workflows.

  • Quiver enables you to quickly visualize and analyze time series and object data through a visual point-and-click interface and a powerful charting library. Without writing any code, you can build and publish analyses and share them with your colleagues to keep everyone on the same page.

  • Contour allows for ad-hoc data analysis on large volume datasets, such as performing joins on 100,000 objects or aggregations on 50,000 rows. Analyze your data in a point-and-click interface and build interactive and sharable dashboards to further explore this data — all without writing any code.

  • Object Explorer makes it easy to drill down into specific objects in the Ontology, both individually and in aggregate. Compare and contrast object sets, perform bulk actions, open them in compatible apps (like Quiver), and export them.

  • Fusion is Foundry’s built-in spreadsheet application. Directly query Foundry datasets and use standard spreadsheet functions to analyze this data. Leverage Fusion’s writeback capabilities to submit results back into Foundry for downstream usage.

  • Vertex allows you to visualize and quantify cause and effect across the digital twin of your real-world organization. Build, curate, and publish graphs for use across your organization or build new system graphs.
