Palantir and STATION F Partner to Empower Early-Stage Companies

STATION F is excited to welcome Palantir Technologies as a partner of the Foster the Future initiative, which will offer workshops and conferences to early-stage companies and allow them to explore its world-class Foundry platform

STATION F, the world’s largest startup campus, and Palantir Technologies Inc., a leading builder of operating systems for the modern enterprise, today announced a year-long partnership to provide STATION F entrepreneurs with access to Palantir’s expertise in data analytics through workshops, events and mentorship.

Palantir will engage with STATION F founders through its “Foster the Future” initiative, offering a number of events focused on how to build data operating systems, along with demonstrations and the ability to interact with Palantir’s Foundry platform. Palantir’s engineers will share their expertise on empowering organizations to effectively integrate their data, decisions, and operations with STATION F’s more than 1,000 startups.

This partnership is born both out of Palantir’s belief that world-class software should be available to early and growth stage companies, as well as its longstanding commitment to working with the public and private sector in Europe. Earlier this year, Palantir introduced Foundry for Builders, a program for startups to license Palantir Foundry under a subscription model. Palantir believes the platform is well suited for fast growing companies as it powers data-driven decision making from the outset and is designed to scale with increasing complexity.

Foundry for Builders now includes more than 20 startups in 4 continents, in fields as varied as manufacturing, entertainment, B2B software, healthcare, financial services, energy, and blockchain. In France, Toulouse-based AURA AERO, a digital and eco-efficient aircraft manufacturer that combines the best of the aeronautical industry and digital technologies, has signed on to be part of the latest cohort of Foundry for Builders.

"Digitalization, a key part in AURA AERO's genesis, is at the heart of our structure and our global performance. We are happy to partner with Palantir in order to elaborate a solid digital strategy to address the challenges of aeronautics and especially those of decarbonated aviation." said Jérémy Caussade, President and Chief Engineer of AURA AERO.

Palantir is also working with some of France’s flagship corporations, including Airbus, Faurecia, and Sanofi, as well as various leading public sector institutions.
