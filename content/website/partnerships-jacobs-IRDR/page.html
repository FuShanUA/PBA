AIP for Infrastructure Resiliency and Disaster Response

AIP for Infrastructure Resiliency and Disaster Response supports Water Utilities, enhancing the preparedness and resiliency of critical water infrastructure assets and expediting the recovery after a disaster.

When faced with a crisis, every moment counts, and disaster response teams often face the challenge of understanding the impact of the event on the system and defining strategies to respond. This response process includes collating information from numerous systems like SCADA, GIS, CMMS, and ERP, which can slow down the pace of critical response efforts. AIP for Infrastructure Resiliency and Disaster Response integrates and unifies data across these disparate systems, offering a comprehensive view (‘Eyes On The System’) that enables operators and executives of the water utilities to effectively understand the issues, the and priorities and coordinate teams and resources to prepare for, respond to, and recover from catastrophic events. By providing an integrated view of the infrastructure system, real and non-real time data and risk assessments, AIP ensures that operators can quickly identify urgent issues and prioritize essential mitigations before, throughout, and after a disaster. Key Features:

  • A Common Operating Platform for Critical Decisions: The Ontology integrates intelligence, geospatial data, and field reports, drawing from SCADA, GIS, CMMS, ERP systems, and ad-hoc utility databases, to offer an integrated and comprehensive view of the data that informs preparedness and early emergency response efforts. A common operating platform unifies the utility response, enhancing communication and collaboration between operating areas.

  • A Digital Twin of Distribution Systems: The Ontology merges real-time sensor data with a digital twin of operational infrastructure, enabling a continued overview of the status of the system, intuitive anomaly detection and simplification of complex sensor data for streamlined decision-making.

  • Early Alerting and Response: The Ontology enables the immediate detection of anomalies in the system behavior and operation (critical alerts), defines their estimated impacts on the customers, and provides criticality analysis to the to field teams, empowering effective triage, prioritization, and response actions.

  • Smart Mitigation Strategies: AIP enables operators to proactively detect infrastructure vulnerabilities ranging from water supply shortages to nearby overflow risks, enabling them to put mitigation plans into action.

  • Readiness for Events: AIP provides a status of the system readiness and gap analysis with defined actions for the operator to close the readiness gap. This may include emergency generators, water trucks, service to critical facilities and their real-time status.
